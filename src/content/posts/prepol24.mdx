**Before we actually get into it, this isn’t your typical PoL.** Though you already know this since I either already said so or because the scene and structure of it is immensely different. Anywho, the purpose of a Pre-POL is to communicate my intentions and my mission for this year *(though I’ll also do a bit of reflecting as to how I’ve been so far)*.

**Over the last week or so, everyone in PLP has been creating a VMV**, which stands for “Vision Mission Values”, which is a document that describes those for the year. My vision, is where I’d like to be at graduation day, my mission is my intentions for this school year that will help me make my vision a reality, and my values which are behaviours I will use to achieve my mission.

Now that you know a bit about my Vision, Mission, and Values for the year, I want to reflect on how I’ve been so far this year as a student. So, we’ve completed a couple of projects this year:

1. For Humanities, Why the World Needs more Canada

2. For PGP (careers), Atomic Habits

3. For Maker, Take a Byte (heavily integrated into Why the World Needs more Canada)

**The first project and most significant project I’m going to talk about is Atomic Habits**, which has probably been my most significant project yet. Our product for this project was an artifact, which **represents our understandings are learnings of Atomic Habits**. My artifact is an NFC Keychain that communicates with my devices to activate a sort of lockdown mode, which adds friction *(makes it more difficult)* for me to do things that aren’t productive on my iPad.

**I’d call this a perfect example of wasted potential.** The concept was solid, and it was cool in theory, but the execution **didn’t quite live up to what I had envisioned**. This mainly came down to my timing and how I managed the project. Over the course of a week, I came up with three different ideas, and this one was my third.

**Even though I only spent around three hours on the final artifact**, I was able to create a functional prototype that fully **met our learning expectations**. Looking back, I can see that with more time and effort, it could’ve reached its **full potential**. Still, I’m proud of what I accomplished in that short period—it’s a strong starting point and a good representation of my ideas.

So basically, I could have put some more effort into it, and it could’ve been better. Though I’m not gonna take this too hardly on myself since at the end of the day this is a good artifact or at least a good prototype that fully met at our learning expectations. It just didn’t meet my own expectations.

**I’d say this artifact was a good representation of my performance in school for the past two months**, basically I’ve been probably putting in around 75 to 80% effort in day-to-day school. Which isn’t a bad thing at all. Which is good in a way, as I have touched on how I don’t wanna be a super try hard 100% student because that’s just gonna lead a lot of stress, anxiety and probably even opposite reactions if I’m just doing school 24/7 and I’m not doing my own stuff.

**My goal isn’t to just be like a 90% or 80% student either.** Like recently a movie called Green Book caught my eye, which I haven’t watched yet for no reason, but one of the characters has a really good mindset that I will live by for myself:

**”Whatever you do, do it 100%. When you work, work; when you laugh, laugh; when you eat, eat like it’s your last meal.” [source](https://www.youtube.com/watch?app=desktop&v=ERttjRmZjm0)**

So, yeah thats sort of the idea that my work will be like this year. Which is still maintaining a steady balance between school and whatnot whilst still being productive on both fronts with time for relaxing.
